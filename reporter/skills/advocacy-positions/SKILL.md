# Advocacy positions (BBUG)

This is the single biggest lever for output coherence. Apply it whenever you draft
asks or the consultation response. Cite positions as `[Position: <id>]`.

## Tone guide
Assertive, evidence-led, technically credible — **not** adversarial for its own
sake. Overstatement undermines our standing with the council; understatement
misses opportunities. Every assertion is backed by a citation. Prefer specific,
draftable asks over general complaint.

## House positions (cite by id)
- `POS-PARKING-01` — Cycle parking must meet **Cherwell parking SPD** minimums as a
  floor, not a target; for residential, secure, covered, ground-level, and
  accessible to **cargo cycles and adapted cycles**, not just standard bikes.
- `POS-LTN120-01` — Any deviation from **LTN 1/20** design parameters must be
  explicitly justified by the applicant; unjustified deviations are an objection
  ground, not a detail to be "agreed later".
- `POS-TRAVELPLAN-01` — A Travel Plan is credible only with **measurable mode-share
  targets, monitoring with teeth, and funded remedial triggers**. A box-tick TP is
  worse than none because it manufactures false comfort.
- `POS-LCWIP-01` — Where a site sits on or adjacent to an **LCWIP route**, the
  application is the delivery mechanism for that route; we ask for delivery or a
  proportionate contribution, not vague support.
- `POS-VAGUE-01` — "could include", "may provide", "to be agreed", "indicative
  only" are not commitments. Ask for them to be **hardened into conditions** or
  treated as absent.

## Red lines (always flag for objection — never auto-decide)
- `RED-01` — Car-dependent development with **no commitment to LCWIP delivery** or
  meaningful off-site active-travel contribution.
- `RED-02` — A primary route to a **school catchment** that is red-rated (LTS-4)
  with no proposed mitigation.
- `RED-03` — Cycle parking below SPD minimum, or provision that excludes
  non-standard cycles.

> Red lines inform the *case for the asks*. They do NOT let you state the headline
> position — that is always the human's call. Surface the red line and its evidence.

## Precedents
Maintain `precedents.md` (past responses to similar applications + outcomes). Check
it before drafting; if we have responded to a related application, that is a
hard-escalation trigger (precedent risk) — say so.
